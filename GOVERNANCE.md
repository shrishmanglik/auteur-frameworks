# Governance

AUTEUR Frameworks is currently maintainer-led.

## Roles

- **Maintainer**: owns releases, roadmap, evidence policy, security response, and final merge decisions.
- **Reviewer**: evaluates a defined technical or creative area and may approve or request changes.
- **Contributor**: proposes issues, documentation, fixtures, tests, or code through the public workflow.

The current maintainer is Shrish Manglik (`@shrishmanglik`). Additional maintainers will be named through a public governance change after sustained, high-quality contribution and review work.

## Decision principles

1. Creator workflow value outranks abstraction for its own sake.
2. Reproducible evidence outranks confident prose.
3. Provider-neutral core stays separate from changing provider capabilities.
4. Private research inputs stay private; generalized frameworks may be public.
5. Breaking changes require migration guidance.
6. Security, licensing, and publication-boundary findings block release.

## Change process

- Bug fixes and documentation changes use a normal pull request.
- New frameworks begin with a framework-proposal issue.
- Breaking schemas, public APIs, evidence classes, or governance rules require a proposal and maintainer approval before implementation.
- Releases require passing local gates, hosted CI, and an independent review for high-risk changes.

## Appeals and conflicts

Contributors may ask the maintainer to reconsider a decision with new technical or evidence-based information. Personal pressure, popularity, or unsupported provider claims do not override the publication and quality gates.
