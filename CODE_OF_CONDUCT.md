# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We are building production infrastructure for creative work. Discussion should be rigorous, specific, respectful, and useful.

Expected behavior:

- critique ideas and evidence rather than people;
- state assumptions and uncertainty;
- give reproducible examples;
- respect creative ownership, privacy, and attribution;
- accept correction when evidence changes;
- help contributors understand the required quality bar.

Unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, threats, discriminatory language, or personal attacks;
- publishing private prompts, media, credentials, customer work, or identifying records;
- claiming ownership of work you cannot license;
- fabricated tests, evidence, capabilities, or benchmarks;
- repeated bad-faith disruption after a maintainer warning.

## Scope

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and project-managed community spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, lock discussions, issue warnings, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior or publication safety violates this standard.

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the repository's security-advisory channel. Include links or screenshots when safe. Maintainers will minimize disclosure and conflicts of interest while reviewing the report.

Good-faith technical disagreement is not a conduct violation. Precision and dissent are welcome; abuse and evidence fabrication are not.
